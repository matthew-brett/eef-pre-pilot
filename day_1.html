Homework¶

  • Download the notebook from the link at top of Three girls - it’s slightly modified solution to the three girls problem we were working on today. Make a copy, that you are going to modify.

  • Modify the notebook to give the probability of 2 girls. What is that?

  • Make another copy of the Notebook for this problem. The actual probability of having a male child is not 0.5 but 0.513. [1]. To do a better simulation, we need to modify the Notebook to give a 0.513 probability of "B" and a 0.487 probability of "G". You can’t easily use random.choice for this, but you could an expression like this to decide if it is a girl:

    random.uniform(0, 1) <= 0.487

    random.uniform(0, 1) returns a random number between 0 and 1 - so the number it returns will be <= 0.487 about 48.7% of the time.

    Modify your new copy of the Notebook to use something like this to give the probability of a girl. You should find that your estimate of the proportion of 3-girl families drops slightly, because girls are slightly less common than boys.

[1]Official UK government statistics give the birth ratio as 105.3. This the number of boys born for every 100 girls.
